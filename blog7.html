Controllers: The Brain of Your App

So Far… Everything Feels Automatic

By now, you’ve seen AngularJS do some pretty cool things: Data appears instantly, inputs update automatically, and there is no refresh needed. It almost feels like things are happening on their own. But behind all of this, there is always something in control. 👉 That “something” is the Controller.

The Chef in the Kitchen 👨‍🍳

Think of your app like a restaurant:

  • The View (HTML) is the Menu. It shows what is available.
  • The $scope is the Waiter. He carries orders back and forth.
  • The Controller is the Chef. He decides how the food is prepared and manages the ingredients (Data).

Controllers Handle Actions

Controllers aren't just for storing names; they handle what happens when a user clicks, types, or submits. They are the decision-makers. Look at this simple counter logic:

// THE BRAIN (Logic)
app.controller("myCtrl", function($scope) {
    $scope.count = 0;

    $scope.increment = function() {
        $scope.count++; // Logic: Add 1 every time
    };
});

<!-- THE FACE (UI) -->
<div ng-controller="myCtrl">
    <p>Current Count: {{ count }}</p>
    <button ng-click="increment()">Click Me</button>
</div>

Why Separation Matters

One of the biggest advantages of Controllers is that they keep JavaScript logic away from HTML design. This means your code stays organized. Designers can focus on making the UI look beautiful in the HTML, while developers focus on the "brains" in the Controller.

⚠️ Pro-Tip: Avoid putting too much logic in your HTML attributes. Keep your real "thinking" inside the Controller!
