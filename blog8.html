Filters: The “Instagram” for Your Data

Ever Looked at Raw Data and Thought… This Looks Ugly?

Imagine showing Price: 1234.5 on your baking website. Technically correct? Yes. User-friendly? Not even close. AngularJS Filters solve this by formatting data without changing the original value.

Raw Data
1234.5
Filtered Data
₹1,234.50

The Instagram Analogy

Think of filters like Instagram. You take one photo (your data), apply a filter (B&W or Bright), and the look changes—but the original photo stays exactly the same. In Angular, it looks like this:

Raw Data → | Filter → Styled Output

Common Filters in Action

The | (Pipe) symbol is the gateway. Your data passes through it to get styled.

<!-- 1. Currency Filter -->
<p>Total: {{ price | currency:"₹" }}</p>

<!-- 2. Text Formatting -->
<p>Name: {{ user.name | uppercase }}</p>

<!-- 3. Sorting Lists -->
<li ng-repeat="x in products | orderBy:'price'">
    {{ x.name }}
</li>
Ma’am’s Favorite Hint ⭐

Always use filters for formatting (currency, date, text). Avoid doing this formatting inside your Controllers—it keeps your logic clean and your code scalable!

Why Developers Love It

Why write 50 lines of JavaScript to format a date when you can do it in 5 characters? Filters separate "how data works" from "how data looks." That is clean architecture.
