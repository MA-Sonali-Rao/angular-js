Expressions & Scope

How Does HTML Talk to JavaScript?

At first, AngularJS feels like magic. You write something in JavaScript… and it just appears in your HTML. But how does the HTML even know what’s happening? The answer lies in two concepts: Expressions and $scope.

$scope: The Messenger 📬

Think of your app as a brain (Controller) and a face (View). $scope is the postman. The Controller writes the letter (data), and $scope carries it to the View to be displayed.

The "Mad Libs" of HTML

Expressions are the placeholders in your HTML. Like a Mad Libs book with blanks to fill in, {{ name }} tells Angular to grab whatever the $scope postman is carrying and drop it right there.

// 1. Controller defines the "letter"
app.controller("myCtrl", function($scope) {
    $scope.name = "Sonali";
});

<!-- 2. HTML uses the "placeholder" -->
<h1>Hello {{ name }}</h1>

Thinking Inside the Braces

Expressions can do more than just show variables—they can calculate! Angular lets your HTML "think" a little by performing math or string combinations on the fly.

  • Math: {{ 5 + 5 }} results in 10
  • Logic: {{ name + " is learning" }} results in "Sonali is learning"

Why This Matters

Before AngularJS, you had to manually connect your logic and your design. Now, they feel like one system. Communication is automatic, and your code stays clean. Remember: $scope sends, {{ }} shows.
